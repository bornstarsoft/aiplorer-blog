Generative artificial intelligence (AI) is experiencing explosive growth in China, with users surpassing 602 million by December 2025. This remarkable increase of 141.7 percent from the previous year highlights the rapid integration of AI into various sectors, marking a significant milestone in China's digital transformation journey. The penetration rate now stands at 42.8 percent, showcasing the widespread adoption of this technology.

During the 14th Five-Year Plan period, generative AI has emerged as a key driver of economic and social change in China. The government, under the leadership of Xi Jinping, has prioritized AI development, leading to a robust ecosystem of over 6,000 AI enterprises and a core industry valued at approximately $173.87 billion. China's commitment to AI research is evident, as it now holds 60 percent of the world's AI-related patents, reflecting its position as a global leader in this field.

As we look to the future, the question remains: how will this rapid advancement in generative AI shape not only China's economy but also the global tech landscape? The potential for innovation is vast, and the world will be watching closely as China continues to push the boundaries of what's possible with AI.
