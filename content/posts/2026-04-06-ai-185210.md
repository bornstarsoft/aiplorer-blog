LG's recent two-day hackathon, the "LG Aimers Hackathon," showcased the company's commitment to nurturing the next generation of tech talent. With 94 young participants selected from over 2,300 applicants, the event focused on optimizing LG's EXAONE large language model, a significant step in advancing AI technologies. This initiative is part of LG's broader goal to train 50,000 young AI professionals by 2030, emphasizing the importance of practical experience in the rapidly evolving tech landscape.

The participants developed 27 lightweight models of EXAONE, demonstrating the potential for AI applications on personal devices. This lightweighting technique not only enhances performance but also makes sophisticated AI accessible to a wider audience. As LG plans to publish these models on Hugging Face, it opens up opportunities for developers globally to leverage this technology.

With a recruitment fair featuring major LG affiliates, the event also provided valuable career opportunities for participants. This holistic approach to education and employment underscores the importance of hands-on experience in tech fields. As we look to the future, how will initiatives like these shape the landscape of AI and tech talent development worldwide?
