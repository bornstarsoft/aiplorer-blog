At the recent World IT Show 2026 in Seoul, South Korea showcased its ambitions in artificial intelligence (AI) through a unique blend of diplomacy and technology. The Ministry of Science and ICT convened industry leaders and diplomats to discuss the future of physical AI, a concept that integrates AI with real-world machines and environments. This initiative highlights Korea's strategic focus on leveraging its advanced manufacturing capabilities to position itself as a global leader in AI.

During a roundtable led by Second Vice Minister Ryu Je-myung, participants from various tech companies and academic institutions identified critical gaps in technology and data deployment. Ryu emphasized the government's commitment to supporting the sector with key technologies and large-scale pilot projects. The event also featured a Global Pavilion, where international companies showcased their innovations, further underscoring the collaborative spirit that Korea aims to foster in the AI landscape.

As Korea strives to become one of the top three AI powers globally, the emphasis on international cooperation and innovation is clear. With the governments backing and a strong industrial base, the future of physical AI in Korea looks promising. Will this initiative pave the way for Korea to lead the AI revolution in the coming years?
