## Goal

Use AI to make a long document easier to understand, while still checking the source material yourself.

Reviewed general assistants such as [ChatGPT](/ai-tools/tools/chatgpt/), [Claude](/ai-tools/tools/claude/), and [Gemini](/ai-tools/tools/gemini/) may be useful for summaries, questions, and follow-up outlines. Features, file support, and availability can change, so check official websites for current details.

## Steps

1. Define the summary you need.
   Ask for key points, action items, open questions, risks, or a plain-language explanation.

2. Provide only material you are allowed to share.
   Do not paste confidential, personal, or restricted information into a tool unless you understand the privacy and policy implications.

3. Ask for a structured summary.
   Example prompt: "Summarize this document in five bullet points, then list important caveats and questions I should verify."

4. Compare the summary with the source.
   Check whether the AI missed conditions, dates, exceptions, numbers, or important context.

5. Ask follow-up questions.
   A second pass can help you find unclear sections, but the document remains the source of truth.
